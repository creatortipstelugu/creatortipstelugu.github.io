Phase 1

Pre-Production

Idea, research, scripting, and preparation before you press record

2–3 Days Before
🔍 Idea & Keyword Research Must Do
  • Search your topic idea on YouTube in Telugu + English to validate demand
    Example: search "ChatGPT Telugu" and "ChatGPT tutorial Telugu" — check if top videos have 10K+ views. High views = proven demand.
  • Use YouTube Search Autocomplete to find exact long-tail keywords
    Type your topic in YouTube's search bar and note all autocomplete suggestions — these are real searches. Include them in title, description & tags.
  • Check Google Trends India to confirm the topic is trending or evergreen
    Visit trends.google.com → set region to India → search your topic. Choose topics with stable or rising interest lines.
  • Identify your primary keyword (1 phrase) and 3–5 secondary keywords for SEO
    Primary keyword goes in title + first line of description. Secondary keywords go in description body and tags. Example: Primary = "Canva tutorial Telugu", Secondary = "Canva for beginners", "free Canva templates".
  • Study the top 3 videos on your topic — note what they covered and what they missed
    Your video should fill a gap — cover something they missed, or explain it better/simpler in Telugu. This is your competitive angle.
  • Confirm the video fits one of your 4 content pillars (AI Tools / Canva / Video Editing / Social Media)
    If it doesn't fit a pillar, assess if it's a one-off or signals a new recurring theme. Avoid random off-topic videos — they confuse the algorithm.
📝 Script & Structure Must Do
  • Write a Hook (first 30 seconds) — state the problem, promise the result, tease what they'll learn
    Example hook: "క్రియేటర్లకు content రాయడానికి గంటలు పడుతుందా? ఈ video చూసిన తర్వాత 10 నిమిషాల్లో complete script తయారవుతుంది — AI తో!"
  • Outline the video in 3–5 clear sections with timestamps planned in advance
    Pre-planned timestamps help you stay on track while recording and make it easier to add chapters on YouTube later.
  • Write or bullet-point the full script or talking points for each section
    For tutorials: write exact steps. For opinion/tip videos: write bullet points you'll expand on. Avoid reading word-for-word — it sounds unnatural.
  • Plan a mid-video CTA (subscribe reminder or community link) around the 40–50% mark
    Research shows mid-video CTAs outperform end-screen CTAs by 3x in click-through rate. Keep it natural: "నీకు ఇది useful గా అనిపిస్తే subscribe చేయి."
  • Plan a strong ending — summary, CTA for next video, and subscribe prompt
    End with: (1) Quick recap in 2–3 sentences, (2) Tease the next video, (3) Direct subscribe ask. Example: "Next week నేను [topic] గురించి explain చేస్తాను — miss అవ్వకు."
  • Identify affiliate links or product mentions to include naturally in the script
    Don't force it — only include links for tools you're actually demonstrating. Plan exactly where in the script to mention them so it feels organic.
⚙️ Technical Setup & Preparation Must Do
  • Set up your recording environment — clean background, good lighting (ring light or window light)
    For screen recordings: ensure your desktop is clean and notifications are turned OFF (Do Not Disturb mode). For face-cam: sit facing a light source.
  • Test audio quality — record a 10-second test and play it back before main recording
    Audio is more important than video quality on YouTube. If it sounds muffled or echoey, fix it before recording. Use a lapel mic if available.
  • Set screen resolution to 1920×1080 (1080p) before any screen recording
    This ensures your final video exports at crisp full HD. Lower resolution screen recordings look blurry when YouTube renders them.
  • Open all apps/websites/tools you'll demonstrate before hitting record
    Avoid watching apps load or waiting for browser tabs during the recording — it kills pacing. Pre-open everything and have sample files/projects ready.
  • Check storage space on your device — ensure you have at least 10 GB free
    Raw screen recordings can be 2–5 GB for a 15-minute video. Running out of storage mid-record corrupts the file.
💡
Pre-Production Shortcut: Keep a running "video ideas" list in Notion or Google Keep. Whenever you discover a new tool, a viewer asks a question, or you see a trending topic, add it immediately. Never start a recording session without a keyword-validated idea.

Phase 2

Production / Recording

Best practices while recording your video to save time in editing

Recording Day
🎙️ Before You Hit Record Must Do
  • Do a 30-second warm-up — speak out loud to warm up your voice before recording
    Read any Telugu text out loud or have a short conversation. Cold recordings sound flat and lack energy.
  • Turn on Do Not Disturb on all devices — phone, computer, and smart watch
  • Close all unnecessary browser tabs and background apps (especially those with audio or notifications)
  • Have your script or bullet points visible on a second screen or printed sheet — NOT on the screen you're recording
🎥 While Recording Must Do
  • Record a clap or loud sound at the start to create an easy sync point if recording audio and video separately
  • Speak 10–15% slower than your natural speed — listeners need time to process Telugu tech vocabulary
    You can speed up in editing. You cannot slow down without robotic distortion. Always record slower than needed.
  • If you make a mistake, pause 3 seconds and redo the sentence — don't stop the recording
    The pause creates a visible spike in your audio waveform, making mistakes easy to find and cut in editing. Much faster than restarting.
  • Zoom into relevant UI elements during screen recordings — use Ctrl + scroll or zoom tools so viewers can clearly read text
  • Move your mouse slowly and deliberately during screen recordings — fast mouse movement confuses viewers
  • Record 15–20 seconds of silence at the beginning and end — useful for adding intro/outro music in editing
  • For face-cam: maintain eye contact with the camera lens, not the screen — it creates a direct connection with the viewer
📦 Shorts Raw Material Pro Move
  • Identify 2–3 standalone tips or moments during recording that could become YouTube Shorts
    Note the timestamp when you say them. Example: "minute 7:30 — the AI prompt trick — good Short." These don't need re-recording, just trimming.
  • Record a vertical format intro (30 seconds, 9:16 aspect ratio) for each Short if possible
    Shorts recorded vertically perform better than cropped horizontal. Record on your phone right after the main recording while the topic is fresh.

Phase 3

Video Editing

Cutting, polishing, adding graphics, captions, and audio treatment

1–2 Days
✂️ Basic Cuts & Structure Must Do
  • Cut all long pauses, filler words (um, uh, అా...) and dead silence longer than 1 second
    This alone typically reduces video length by 20–30% and dramatically improves pacing and watch time.
  • Add a branded intro (5–8 seconds max) — logo animation or your channel's standard opener
    Keep it short. Viewers skip intros longer than 8 seconds. The actual hook should start at second 0, with the intro bumper at the very end of the hook.
  • Add section title cards between major sections to visually organize the video
    Example: "Step 1: Install the Tool", "Step 2: Basic Settings", "Pro Tip". These also become chapter timestamps.
  • Add a branded outro (10–15 seconds) with end screen — include subscribe button and link to related video
  • Verify the final video length is appropriate: 8–15 min for tutorials, 5–8 min for tip videos
    Videos under 8 minutes earn less AdSense (no mid-roll ads). Over 15 minutes risks drop-off. Sweet spot: 10–13 minutes for your content type.
🔊 Audio Treatment Must Do
  • Apply noise reduction on your voice track to remove background hiss or fan noise
    CapCut has a free "Noise Reduction" filter. In DaVinci Resolve, use the Voice Isolation or Noise Reduction effect. Apply subtly — over-processing sounds robotic.
  • Normalize audio volume so your voice stays between -12 dB and -6 dB throughout
    Uneven volume (loud in some places, quiet in others) is the #1 reason viewers use headphones and then abandon a video. Keep it consistent.
  • Add background music at low volume (10–15% of voice volume) — use copyright-free sources only
    Free sources: YouTube Audio Library, Pixabay Music, Epidemic Sound (paid). Music should be soft and non-distracting — lo-fi or ambient works best for tutorials.
  • Add sound effects for transitions, button clicks, or emphasis moments (optional but adds production quality)
💬 Captions & Text Must Do
  • Add Telugu subtitles/captions to the video — auto-generate and correct errors
    YouTube's auto-captions for Telugu are improving but still error-prone. Review and fix. Captions keep viewers watching in silent mode (commuters, public spaces).
  • Highlight key terms, tool names, and important steps with on-screen text overlays
    When you say "ఇక్కడ Settings menu లో API Key enter చేయాలి" — show "API Key" as a text callout on screen. This reinforces learning for non-native English readers.
  • Add a subscribe reminder graphic at the 40–50% mark of the video (animated bell icon or banner)
  • Add zoom effects on important UI elements during screen recordings so they're clearly visible
    Use CapCut's zoom/magnify effect or manually scale the clip. Buttons and menu items that look tiny on your screen look microscopic to viewers.
🎞️ Export Settings Must Do
  • Export at 1920×1080 (1080p), 30fps (or 60fps for smooth screen content)
  • Export format: MP4 with H.264 codec — optimal file size to quality ratio for YouTube
    Target file size: 1–3 GB for a 10–15 min video. Larger files take longer to upload and process but quality is marginally better — not worth it.
  • Watch the full exported video once before uploading — check audio sync, look for visual glitches
    It takes 10–15 minutes but saves you from the embarrassment of re-uploading. At least watch the first 2 minutes and random segments throughout.
⚡ Shorts Editing Pro Move
  • Create 2 Shorts from the main video — crop to 9:16 vertical, trim to 30–60 seconds
    Best Short material: the single best tip, a surprising result, or a quick "before/after" transformation. The first 2 seconds must immediately grab attention.
  • Add large-font captions to Shorts — 80% of Shorts are watched without sound on mobile
  • Add a "Watch full video" text/card at the end of each Short to drive traffic to the main video

Phase 4

Thumbnail Creation

The most important click-driver — your thumbnail is your billboard

Same Day as Edit
🖼️ Thumbnail Design (in Canva) Must Do
  • Create thumbnail at exactly 1280 × 720 px in Canva
  • Include your face with a strong, relevant emotion — surprise, excitement, or pointing at the result
    Thumbnails with human faces get 38% higher CTR on average. Avoid neutral/blank expressions — they don't communicate emotion or urgency.
  • Add 3–5 bold words maximum in Telugu and/or English — readable at mobile thumbnail size (120px wide)
    Test by zooming out to 25% in Canva — if you can still read the text, it's large enough. Use a bold condensed font at minimum 80px.
  • Use high-contrast colors — the thumbnail should pop against YouTube's white and dark backgrounds
    Yellow, red, and orange on dark backgrounds have the highest contrast. Avoid pastel-on-white — it's invisible in feeds.
  • Include a visual result or screenshot of what the viewer will achieve by watching
    For AI tools: show the AI-generated output. For Canva: show the final design. For video editing: show the before/after. Results-driven thumbnails beat concept thumbnails every time.
  • Use your channel's consistent brand colors and font — viewers should recognize your thumbnails in 0.5 seconds
    Pick 2–3 brand colors and 1 font style. Use them in every thumbnail. Brand recognition in feeds is what converts casual viewers into subscribers.
  • Export thumbnail as JPG, under 2 MB file size
  • Check thumbnail on your phone screen — if it looks good at mobile size, it looks good everywhere
🧪 Thumbnail Testing Pro Move
  • Create 2 different thumbnail versions — different color scheme or different text angle
    Use YouTube's built-in A/B test feature (YouTube Studio → Video → Test & Compare) to find the winner after 48 hours. Swap to the winner permanently.
  • Ask in your Telegram/WhatsApp community which thumbnail they'd click — quick community poll
🏆
Telugu Thumbnail Tip: Add the word "Telugu లో" or "తెలుగు" visibly on your thumbnail. Telugu-speaking viewers actively scan thumbnails for language signals — this can boost your CTR by 20–40% within your niche audience.

Phase 5

Upload & YouTube SEO

Optimize every metadata field so YouTube can find and recommend your video

Upload Day
✍️ Title Optimization SEO Critical
  • Start the title with your primary keyword in Telugu (the most-searched phrase)
    Formula: [Topic] Telugu లో | [Benefit/Result] [Year] — Example: "ChatGPT Telugu లో | Content Writing 10 నిమిషాల్లో 2025"
  • Keep title under 70 characters — longer titles get truncated in search results
  • Include a number, power word, or year in the title for higher CTR
    Words that boost CTR: "Complete", "Free", "Beginners", "2025", "Secret", "Step-by-Step", numbers like "5 tools", "10 minutes".
  • Use pipe ( | ) to separate the main title from a secondary hook
    Example: "Canva Tutorial Telugu | Beginners Complete Guide 2025". The second part catches viewers who search differently.
📄 Description Optimization SEO Critical
  • Write a compelling first 2 lines (150 characters) with primary keyword — this is what shows in search results
    Example: "ఈ video లో Canva తో professional YouTube thumbnails ఎలా design చేయాలో step-by-step Telugu లో నేర్చుకోండి."
  • Add video chapters/timestamps using 0:00 - Section Name format
    Chapters improve watch time (viewers jump to relevant parts instead of leaving), boost SEO, and show up as chapter previews in Google search.
  • Include all affiliate links with clear labels — Canva, tools demonstrated, etc.
    Always add: "🔗 Canva Pro Free Trial: [your affiliate link]". Disclose it's an affiliate link as required by YouTube policy.
  • Add links to your Telegram, WhatsApp Community, and Instagram
  • Naturally include secondary keywords (3–5 times each) in the description body
    Don't stuff — write naturally. "ఈ tutorial లో AI tools for content creators, ChatGPT prompts, and Telugu digital tools explain చేశాను."
  • Add a standard boilerplate footer to every description — About Me, channel links, contact email
    Save this as a template in Notion so you paste it in every video without retyping. Saves 5 minutes per upload.
🏷️ Tags, Cards & Settings SEO Critical
  • Add 15–20 tags — primary keyword first, then variations, then broader category tags
    Tags: exact phrase → variations → broader. Example: "Canva Tutorial Telugu" → "Canva Beginners Telugu" → "Canva Tutorial" → "Graphic Design Telugu" → "Content Creator Telugu".
  • Set the correct video Category — "Howto & Style" or "Science & Technology" for your content
  • Add the video to the relevant Playlist (AI Tools, Canva, Video Editing, or Social Media)
    Playlists increase session watch time as YouTube auto-plays the next video. Always add to at least one playlist.
  • Add 2 info cards at relevant moments — link to a related video and to your most-watched video
    Place cards at points where viewers might leave (after a key tip is delivered) to redirect them to another video rather than lose them.
  • Add end screen elements — Subscribe button, 2 video recommendations, in the last 20 seconds
  • Upload custom captions/subtitle file if you have them, or enable auto-captions and select Telugu
  • Set Audience to "Not made for kids" and confirm no age restriction is needed
  • Disclose any paid promotions or affiliate content using YouTube's built-in disclosure checkbox
  • Schedule the video for your consistent upload day and time (recommended: Wednesday 6–8 PM IST)
    Consistency in upload schedule trains both the algorithm and your audience. 6–8 PM IST has peak Telugu-speaking viewer activity.
🔍
SEO Multiplier: After uploading, search your exact video title on Google within 24 hours. If it appears in Google Search results (which it will for Telugu content), share that Google Search link in your Telegram community — it adds a powerful backlink signal.

Phase 6

Post-Publish Promotion

Distribution, community building, and boosting the video in the first 48 hours

First 48 Hours
📣 Immediate Distribution (First 2 Hours) Growth Critical
  • Post the video link in your Telegram channel with a 2-line hook (not just the link)
    Write: "New video out! ఈ video లో [key benefit]. Click చేసి చూడండి 👇 [link]". A compelling message gets 3x more clicks than just the link.
  • Share in WhatsApp communities and groups — your own community first, then relevant Telugu groups
    Do NOT spam every group with every video. Share only in groups where the topic is directly relevant. Share as a message, not just a forwarded link.
  • Post the video (or a 30-sec clip) to Instagram Reels with telugu hashtags and bio link
    Hashtags to use: #TeluguTech #TeluguCreator #AIToolsTelugu #CanvaTelugu #DigitalMarketingTelugu
  • Post a Community tab update on YouTube — announce the video with the thumbnail image
    Community posts notify subscribers who have bell turned off. This is a secondary notification system — don't skip it.
  • Share in 2–3 relevant Telugu Facebook groups with a question or context, not just the link
    Example post: "AI తో content writing చేయడం easy అవుతుందా? నేను try చేసాను — results surprising గా ఉన్నాయి. Full video ఇక్కడ చూడండి 👇"
💬 Engagement (First 48 Hours) Growth Critical
  • Reply to every single comment within 24 hours of publishing
    This signals to YouTube that your video has high engagement. The algorithm rewards videos where viewers comment AND the creator responds — it shows community health.
  • Pin a comment from yourself asking a question or sharing a key takeaway to drive comment engagement
    Example: "మీకు ఈ tool useful గా అనిపించిందా? Comment లో చెప్పండి! 👇 Also, next video గురించి topic suggest చేయండి." Questions generate 4x more comments than statements.
  • Heart/like the best comments in the first 24 hours to reward engaged viewers
  • Upload the 2 Shorts from this video (if not already done) — space them out: 1 on publish day, 1 two days later
🔁 Cross-Platform Repurposing Pro Move
  • Write a LinkedIn post summarizing the top 3 learnings from the video (targeting Telugu professionals)
    LinkedIn Telugu creator content is extremely underserved. A single viral LinkedIn post can add 50–100 subscribers from a completely new audience segment.
  • Create a story on Instagram with a swipe-up link or "link in bio" CTA for the new video
  • If applicable, share the video in any Reddit communities or Discord servers for Telugu creators or tech enthusiasts

Phase 7

Analytics & Optimization Review

Learn from each video to make the next one perform better — review weekly & monthly

48hrs + 7 Days After
📊 48-Hour Performance Check Must Do
  • Check Click-Through Rate (CTR) — target is above 4% for established channels, 2–3% is okay when starting
    If CTR is below 2%: change the thumbnail immediately. YouTube will show your new thumbnail to the next batch of impressions.
  • Check Average View Duration (AVD) — target is above 40% of total video length
    If AVD is below 30%, review the audience retention graph to find the exact drop-off point. That moment is either boring, confusing, or too long.
  • Check traffic sources — what percentage is from YouTube Search vs Suggested vs External?
    High Search traffic = SEO is working. High Suggested traffic = algorithm is recommending you. Low of both = promote more externally and revisit keywords.
  • If CTR is below 2.5%, swap the thumbnail to your alternate version and test for another 48 hours
📅 Weekly Analytics Habit Must Do
  • Every Sunday: review the top-performing video of the week — what made it work?
    Look at: which traffic source drove the most views, what the CTR was, where retention peaked. Replicate what worked in your next video.
  • Note the exact drop-off points in the Audience Retention graph for this week's video
    Common drop-off causes: too slow intro (fix: cut intro by 30%), long tangents (fix: stay tighter to the topic), unrelated section (fix: remove it).
  • Check subscriber count growth — which video drove the most new subscribers this week?
    The top subscriber-driving video reveals what your audience truly wants. Plan more content in that exact style and topic area immediately.
  • Review top comments and questions — pick 1 comment that becomes next week's video topic
    Viewer questions are pre-validated video ideas. When someone asks "ఇది free లో చేయగలమా?" — that's your next video title: "Free లో [topic] ఎలా చేయాలి".
🗓️ Monthly Deep Review Pro Move
  • Compare Watch Time hours vs last month — track progress toward 4,000 hours YPP threshold
  • Review affiliate link clicks and conversions — which videos drove the most affiliate income?
  • Identify your top 3 performing videos and plan sequels, updated versions, or related topics
  • Review your content pillar balance — are you posting evenly across AI, Canva, Editing, and Social Media?
  • Set next month's content calendar — 4 main videos + 8 Shorts minimum, topics keyword-validated
📈
The 1% Improvement Rule: After each video, identify one specific thing to improve — audio quality, thumbnail, hook, or pacing. Improving 1% per video compounds dramatically. After 50 videos, your content quality will be unrecognizable from where you started.
